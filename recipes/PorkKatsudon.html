Pork Katsudon

You might not want to be a pork cutlet, but with this recipe you can totally eat a pork cutlet! And, really, what could be more desirable than a steaming bowl of rice, breaded pork cutlet, onions, and egg. Funnily enough, katsu and the word for victory are pronounced the same in Japanese (カツ and 勝つ, respectively), so athletes will often eat some katsudon before or after they compete. So, in honor if Yuri Katsuki’s birthday, make some tasty katsudon!

Ingredients

  • 1 Pork cutlet (2cm thick)
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp plain flour
  • 1 Tsp water
  • 1 Egg
  • Panko breadcrumbs
  • Canola oil
  • 1 1/2 Tbsp Soy Sauce
  • 1/2 Tbsp Sake
  • 1/2 small white onion (sliced)
  • Steamed rice

Instructions

  1. Score pork to prevent curling, season with salt and pepper, then dredge in flour, egg wash, and panko until fully coated.
  2. Deep fry the pork for 3 minutes on each side until golden, then rest on a rack over paper towels.
  3. Simmer dashi, soy sauce, brown sugar, mirin, sake, and onion for 3 minutes until the onion softens.
  4. Slice pork, place over sauce, pour beaten eggs on top, cover, and cook until eggs are set.
  5. Serve pork, egg, and sauce over hot steamed rice, topped with peas and spring onion.
