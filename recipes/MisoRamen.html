Miso Ramen

Miso Ramen is a rich, savory Japanese noodle soup made with a broth base of fermented soybean paste (miso). It’s known for its deep umami flavor and creamy texture, often paired with toppings like sliced pork (chashu), soft-boiled eggs, corn, bamboo shoots, and green onions. Originating in Hokkaido, miso ramen is especially comforting during cold weather and is beloved for its hearty and satisfying taste.

  • 2 Cloves Minced Garlic
  • 1 Tablespoon Vegetable oil
  • 3 Inch knob of ginger
  • 50 ml Soy Sauce
  • 30 ml Hon mirin
  • 50 ml Cooking Sake
  • 180 g White miso
  • 130 g Sweet Shiro Miso
  • 7 Cups Water
  • 200 ml Katsuobushi (Bonito Flakes, Optional)
  • 2 Slices tofu Chashu
  • Sesame seeds
  • Slices green onions

  1. Soak kombu, bonito flakes, anchovies, and 7 cups of water for 30–60 mins. Heat gently (do not boil) for 10 mins until kombu is soft and slimy.
  2. Heat water, soy sauce, mirin, 1 shiitake, spring onion, and grated ginger (don’t boil). Use 600ml to marinate 2 soft-boiled eggs; marinate silken tofu in the rest.
  3. Strain the dashi, transfer to a large pot, add shiitake, onions, garlic, cabbage, salt, carrots, leek, cashews, and daikon. Simmer for 1 hour, then remove solids.
  4. Fry shallot, ginger, and garlic in oil until golden; remove solids. Sauté fresh garlic and ginger, then add soy sauce, mirin, sake, two types of miso, and sesame seeds.
  5. Sear chashu until browned and slice. In a bowl, combine 200ml broth, 75–100ml oat milk, and 1.5 tbsp tare. Add cooked noodles, 1 tbsp aroma oil, toppings, chashu, and egg.
